Skincare Routine

Now that you've figured out your skin type, you can learn what types of products you should use. The basic skincare products someone would use are: A Normal Cleanser, Moisturizer, and Sunscreen. More fancier products include: Exfoliants,Toner/Essence, Face Sprays, Retinols, Eye cream, Serums, and more. The order of products you should use and some defintions are:

    1. Cleaning Balm/Oil (Optional)
    2. Normal Cleanser
    3. Toner/Essence (Optional)
    4. Serum (Optional)
    5. Exfoliants (Optional)
    6. Eye Cream (Use before Retinol)
    7. Retinols (Optional)
    8. Eye Cream (If not using Retinols)
    9. Moisturizer
    10. Sunscreen
    11. Face Spray
Cleaning Balm/Oil:
Oil-based Makeup/Sunscreen Remover
Toner/Essence:
Products that have a consistency of water, provides hydration
Serum:
Products that have active ingredients. "Shots" of treatment
Retinols :
Form of Vitamin A, topically applied on the skin
Face Spray:
A product you spray on your face

Oily and Dry Skin Reccomendations

People with Oily Skin should avoid rich creams, and intense hydration because this could cause your skin to feel really greasy and heavy. Instead, try opting for lighter moisturizers and toners so that your skin still feels well hydrated. Oily skinned people tend to be prone to acne and clogged pores, so I would reccomend trying BHA to clear out all the bacteria causing you acne. I would also reccomend Cleansing Balms/Oils, which remove makeup and grime of your skin, to deeply cleanse the insides of your pores. Oils attract other oils which help dissolve makeup, sunscreens, and excess sebum off our skin. If you have Dry Skin, you should avoid the opposite of Oily skinned people. You want rich creams that deliver intense moisture to your skin so that you won't experience tightness, and irritation. Personally, I would reccomend using essence rather than toner, because essences are usually more thicker than toners. Dry skinned people tend to not experience breakouts or acne, maybe just an occasional pimple or two, I would not reccomend using harsh retionols or exfoliants. If you really want to, use them in moderation and build up your tolerance.
